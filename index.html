Luminance Compensation MEMC for Video Frame Interpolation
May 2021 - May 2022
Publication : Z. Xu, W. Ying, H. He, Q. Zhu, J. Liang and H. Wang, "Luminance Compensation MEMC for Video Frame Interpolation," in IEEE Access, vol. 10, pp. 120752-120764, 2022, doi: 10.1109/ACCESS.2022.3222525.
Paper Link : https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/9953108

Assisted in the composition of the paper Luminance Compensation MEMC for Video Frame Interpolation. This paper is published on IEEE Access.

This paper proposes a video interpolation algorithm based on luminance compensation MEMC (LC-MEMC). Firstly, a luminance compensation method based on the electromagnetic irradiation attenuation in human tissue is introduced to improve the performance of motion estimation and motion compensation (MEMC) and reduce matching errors caused by luminance leap. Secondly, LC-MEMC proposes an improved block matching approach, including i) a new search method from basic points to local points and ii) a block matching criterion that simplifies the calculation process. LC-MEMC improves the accuracy and processing speed of video interpolation from three perspectives: adding luminance compensation, improving the search strategy and optimizing the matching degree calculation method for each search position. We evaluated LC-MEMC on collected medical videos and achieved higher accuracy, faster processing speed, and significantly better viewing experience comparing with existing methods.

PET-STORY: An AI Supported System for Recording Pets behaviors and Creating Pet Stories
May 2021 - May 2022

Implemented a browser based software with Streamlit that deploys a pipeline of deep-learning models to extracts actions and sentiments from visual media and generates articles.

To keep records about pets’ lives and understand them better, pet owners generally take photos or videos. However, photos or videos without text description are low-level information which may lead to inefficient recording. What’s more, lacking semantic and emotional tags affects understanding of the pets‘ intensions and the interaction efficiency because of the ‘gulf’ problem in Animal Computer Interaction. In this study, we first built Pet-Story, an AI supported system extracting pet-related objects and actions as semantic tags. Furthermore, we proposed an interactive co-creation AI system for adding emotional tags and detailed notes to photos or videos. Through our summative studies of pet owners (N=15) and people who do not raise pets (N=10) , 86.7% pet owners enjoyed getting descriptions of pets behaviors from photos or videos with our system more than by themselves. 73.3% pet owners indicated that Pet--Story can help them better understand pets behavior. Pet--Story showed paths to improvement of Animal Computer Interaction.

Intro to Comp Sys (CMU 15213/15513) Labs
Summer 2022

The labs of Intro to Computer System (15513) are very high quality. I've learned a lot about how different systems work in the computer through the simulations of these labs. The following are the four largest and complicated labs of 15513:

Cache Lab: Understanding Cache Memories. The lab consists of three parts: 1) write several traces to test the behavior of a cache simulator, 2) write a small C program that simulates the behavior of a hardware cache memory, and 3) optimize a matrix transpose function, with the goal of minimizing the number of cache misses. Here, I used the idea of blocking.

Malloc Lab: Writing a Dynamic Storage Allocator. I wrote a correct, efficient, and fast dynamic memory allocator (similar to malloc functionality in C) which consist of the malloc, free, realloc, and calloc functions. Here, I used the idea of segmental explicit free list (with anonymous struct and anonymous union) to maintain the free chuncks, coalescing neibouring free chuncks, first fit, eliminated footer of allocated chuncks by adding a flag bit in the header to indicate previous allocation status, and maintianed a seperate list for mini blocks (16KB).

Shell Lab: Writing Your Own Linux Shell. The purpose of this assignment is to utilize the concepts of process control and signalling by writing a simple Linux shell program, tsh (tiny shell), that supports a simple form of job control and I/O redirection. Tsh is an interactive command-line interpreter that runs programs on behalf of the user. It repeatedly prints a prompt, waits for a command line on stdin, and then carries out some action, as directed by the contents of the command line. Built-in commands run within the shell’s process. The child processes created as a result of interpreting a single command line can be a foreground job or background job. In this lab, I played with signal handlers a lot. For example, handling ended backgroud jobs correctly without interuption the foreground jobs, getting backgroud jobs to foreground, switching job status, etc.

Proxy Lab: Writing a Caching Web Proxy. In this lab I created a sequential web proxy which can get request, parse request header and handle errors. I then extended my proxy to deal with multiple concurrent requests and caching web objects.
